# A fictional archival interview for deterministic tests

Interviewer: Why should a research archive preserve original evidence?

Fictional archivist: Preservation makes later corrections possible. A transformed copy can improve readability, but it cannot replace the original record. Reviewers need both provenance and a clear account of every transformation.

Interviewer: What should happen when the record is incomplete?

Fictional archivist: The archive should say so. A careful refusal is more useful than a confident answer that exceeds the evidence. This fictional statement is test data and is not attributed to any real founder.
